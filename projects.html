Uncovering Driver Motivation for Disengagement

UW-Madison CSL

Synopsis: Understanding why drivers transfer control of an Automated Vehicle (AV) can lead to a better ride experience. As the car learn's the driver's driving style, it can adapt and create a trustworthy relationship with the driver. What happens if we give driver's a couple of risky road scenarios? Will they trust the vehicle to drive itself or will they choose to take control? What happens when we give the driver a secondary task to complete while the car drives? What about in multiple weather scenarios? Different ways to transfer control? These are some of the questions we are trying to answer in the WIP paper @ UW-Madison CSL. I want to complete this paper and submit to The Journal of Advanced Transportation.

BERT v SVM: Multi-Label Text Classification of Long Documents

Synopsis: How should we label documents for easy retrieval? This question appears easy on the surface, but quickly becomes a nightmare with a large document database. In this project I worked on as a Machine Learning Research Engineer @ MBZUAI, I was able to see the different ways to assign labels to documents greater than 512 tokens. We found that this is not a task for deep learning. Support Vector Machines can do this work using bag-of-words technique at a fraction of the cost and perform better on all datasets. This project showed me the state-of-the-art deep learning models are not the fix for every problem. Knowing when to deploy the correct model becomes an important task that every ML Engineer should know.
