Mohamed bin Zayed University of Artificial Intelligence

Advisor: Prof. Chih-Jen Lin | Distinguished Professor @ National Taiwan University | Affiliated Professor @ MBZUAI

Summer 2023

As a member of the first UGRIP cohort, I focused on Extreme Multi-Label & Long Document Text Classification. Current deep learning methods utilize pretrained models such as BERT to process texts, however with a 512 token limit, vital context may be lost. We want to process large documents, but BERT and it's attention mechanism may not be the best option. In fact, linear classifiers are extremely competitive and often outperform deep learning models in accuracy and speed.

Deep Learning is NOT the golden ticket

I attended UGRIP around the time ChatGPT made it's grand entrance. The internet was abuzz about it's amazing capability, and I too was impressed. Having this power so accessible was a game changer and it seemed as though it was the closest to an AGI we had ever gotten. My project couldn't have come at a better time. Seeing first hand the downfalls of massive models and the strength of their predecessors was so important as a rising ML Engineer. The speed turned training from turn hours of computing time into mere seconds. Additionally, these SVM's can be run on CPU, providing an alternative to the in-demand GPU's. At the end of the day, these deep learning models are amazing, but they aren't for every task. Learning when to employ the correct model is not only important, but ethical.
